Humans

Multicellular organisms; cells with a nucleus, with cell membranes but lacking cell walls

Phylum: Chordata

Animals with a spinal cord

Class: Mammalia

  • Warm-blooded chordates that bear live young;
  • Have hair or fur;
  • females have mammary glands that secrete milk to nourish young

Order: Primates

  • Mammals with collar bone;
  • eyes face forward;
  • grasping hands with fingers;
  • two types of teeth (incisors and molars)
Family: Hominidae
  1. Primates with upright posture
  2. different use of hands and feet
Genus: Homo

Hominids with S-curved spine, recognisable as human

Species: Homo sapiens

Humans with high forehead, well-developed chin, thin skull bones
